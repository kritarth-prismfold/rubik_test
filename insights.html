Email Migration
Manufacturing · 600 Users · Ahmedabad

Zero-Downtime Email Migration for a Mid-Market Manufacturing Firm

The Challenge: A 600-user manufacturing organisation was operating a legacy on-premise email infrastructure with recurring performance issues, storage limitations, and no disaster recovery capability. The organisation required a migration to Microsoft 365 with zero data loss and minimal operational disruption.

Zero Data Loss Under 2 Hours Downtime 99.99% Post-Migration Uptime

What We Did

Rubik Infotech conducted a pre-migration audit of the existing mail environment, mapped mailbox dependencies, and designed a phased migration plan using Microsoft's hybrid configuration. The migration was executed over a weekend window with real-time monitoring and a rollback protocol in place.

Key steps included a pilot migration of 50 users to validate the process, a full dependency audit identifying shared calendars and distribution groups, and a communications plan for all 600 users that minimised confusion during the cutover period.

Outcome

The migration completed with zero data loss and under 2 hours of total downtime. The organisation gained cloud-native email, integrated Microsoft Teams and OneDrive access, and a fully documented recovery playbook for future resilience.

Post-migration, the IT team reported a significant reduction in email-related support tickets, and the organisation was able to retire two on-premise servers, reducing infrastructure costs by approximately 18% annually.

Workforce Analytics & Licence Optimisation
KPO Services · 1,200 Seats · Pune

Workforce Productivity and Licence Optimisation for a KPO Services Provider

The Challenge: A KPO with 1,200 seats was running multiple overlapping productivity tools — Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and several independent SaaS subscriptions — without a unified view of utilisation or spend. Licence costs were escalating without measurable productivity return.

340 Licences Recovered 28% Cost Reduction Full Compliance Achieved

What We Did

Rubik Infotech conducted a full software asset inventory and utilisation audit across all deployed tools. Using telemetry data and active directory exports, we identified 340 redundant licences assigned to inactive or departed users, and a further 180 licences where users had duplicate access across two platforms.

We then designed a consolidated productivity stack — migrating the organisation to a single Microsoft 365 Enterprise deployment — and implemented a licence governance framework with monthly reporting, onboarding automation, and a 30-day licence reclamation policy for inactive accounts.

Outcome

The organisation reduced its annual software spend by 28% and achieved full licence compliance — eliminating exposure to vendor audit risk. The consolidated Microsoft 365 environment improved IT manageability and provided data-driven insights for future headcount planning.

The licence governance framework continues to deliver value — the organisation reports that IT overhead related to software provisioning has reduced by approximately 40% since the new onboarding and offboarding processes were implemented.

Managed Security Operations
Pharmaceuticals · 8 Sites · 3,500 Employees · Mumbai

Managed Security Operations for a Multi-Site Pharmaceutical Group

The Challenge: A pharmaceutical group with 8 manufacturing sites and 3,500 employees had no centralised security operations capability. Security events were handled reactively by IT staff without dedicated tooling, defined processes, or escalation paths. The group was exposed to significant compliance risk under Schedule M requirements.

80% Reduction in Alert Fatigue 8 Sites Integrated Schedule M Audit Readiness Achieved

What We Did

Rubik Infotech designed and deployed a centralised SIEM solution integrated with endpoint telemetry, network logs, and cloud activity feeds across all 8 manufacturing sites. The deployment used a hub-and-spoke architecture to aggregate log data at the central security operations function while maintaining site-level visibility.

We implemented structured detection rules calibrated to the pharmaceutical threat profile — prioritising anomalous access to manufacturing systems, data exfiltration attempts, and OT network anomalies. Incident response playbooks were developed for 12 high-priority scenarios and tested with tabletop exercises before go-live.

Outcome

Within 90 days, the group had full visibility across all sites, automated alerting for high-priority events, and a documented incident response capability. Alert fatigue was reduced by 80% through tuned detection rules, eliminating the noise that had previously caused security events to go uninvestigated.

The group achieved audit readiness for Schedule M compliance within the project timeframe, satisfying the documentation and process control requirements that had been identified as gaps in their previous audit cycle. Rubik Infotech continues to provide managed security services to the group under an ongoing MSSP agreement.
